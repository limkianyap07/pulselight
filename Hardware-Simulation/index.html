Smart Piezoelectric Street Lighting

Four high-footfall zones in Bukit Bintang each host a piezoelectric tile array that harvests energy from pedestrian footsteps. A central hub aggregates voltage and distributes smart lighting across all zones — adapting brightness to real-time traffic, weather, and time of day.

Notes & Design Rationale

📍Zone A · Pavilion KL Crossing — highest footfall during shopping hours. Piezo tiles generate peak voltage during peak retail hours (10am–10pm).
🚇Zone B · Lot 10 / MRT Entrance — consistent pedestrian flow tied to Bukit Bintang MRT station. Morning and evening rush provides high and predictable energy generation.
🍜Zone C · Jalan Alor Entrance — peak activity from 6pm–midnight during hawker hours. Weather-sensitive: rain reduces footfall significantly.
🏙️Zone D · Fahrenheit88 Walkway — covered walkway, weather-resilient. Moderate but consistent footfall. Good backup source during rainy conditions.
⚡Energy scaling: Each pedestrian generates ~0.5–1.5V per step. 8 pedestrians on-tile simultaneously can produce up to 11V. The MT3608 boost converter steps this up to stable 5V DC for the Arduino.
🌧️Weather mode: Rain increases reflectivity demand for LED brightness (safety). Sunny day mode dims LEDs by 30% to conserve energy when ambient light is high.
